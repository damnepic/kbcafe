End-of-page continuation

Six ways to end a lesson so the reader keeps going instead of leaving. Each is shown as it would appear right after the last paragraph of an article. Hover the buttons to feel the interaction.

The goal: more pageviews and more time on site, by always offering one obvious, low-friction next move. The enemy is the dead end, an article that just… stops. Today most pages end in a flat list of related links; these are stronger.
1Path progress + the next stepRecommended▾

When the page belongs to a learning path, show how far along they are and hand them the very next step as a big dark button. Turns a single read into a sequence.

…so the model can suddenly read your code, query your database, and search the web, all in one conversation. That is the whole point of MCP.
