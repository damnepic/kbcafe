Share iconography

Six directions for a uniform, systematic share row. The brief: keep the recognizable social brand marks, and stop the SHARE control from reading like a separate text label, make it part of the icon family while still signalling “share this, lots of ways.”

The colour system used below (consistent across every concept): platform icons carry their own brand colour X Reddit LinkedIn Facebook; our own actions (email, copy link) carry KB Cafe coral . The share launcher is always the tray + up-arrow (or connected-nodes) glyph, never the word competing inside the row. Hover any icon to feel the interaction.
1Anchor chip + hairline dividerRecommended

The share launcher becomes a chip the exact size and shape of the icons, holding the tray glyph on a soft coral-tinted fill. A 1px divider after it groups the row as “one launcher, then the destinations.”

SHARE fix: same chip dimensions as every icon, so it blends into the family, but the tinted fill + divider make it unmistakably the “start here” control. Best balance of uniform and legible.

2Silent share (icon-only, label on hover)

Round icons throughout. The launcher drops the word entirely and relies on the universally understood tray glyph, outlined in ink so it leads without shouting. The word “Share” appears only as a tooltip on hover.

3Segmented share bar

All controls live inside a single rounded container, like a toolbar or segmented control. The launcher is the first segment, set off by a faint inset divider. The container itself communicates “this whole thing is for sharing.”

5Brand-filled circles + node glyph

Bold and unmissable: solid brand-coloured circles. The launcher is a coral circle carrying the connected-nodes glyph, the clearest possible “send this outward to many places.” Same circle size as the rest, so it sits in the family despite leading the action.
